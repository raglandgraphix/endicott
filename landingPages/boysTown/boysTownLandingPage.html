Boys Town Educational Center

Thin Brick by Endicott

Architect: DLR Group

Precaster: Enterprise Precast Concrete

After years of collaboration, one of the largest non-profit childcare organizations in the country, Boys Town has opened its Education Center as a beacon of hope for students who have faced adversity. Founded in 1917 by Fr. Edward J. Flanagan, as a refuge for homeless boys, Boys Town has grown to be much more. Boys Town no offers services to children and families regardless of gender. In addition to the supportive residential care for children it offers mental health resources, educational services, and vocational training. The architect used playful brick patterns that break up large wall spans with intricate design, the exterior pays homage to other historic Boys Town facilities, yet stand out as state of the art, providing a symbol of strength, stability, and opportunity. Brick was chosen because of its unique blend of materials that gives them a distinctive texture and color. The bricks of this three-story, 110,000 square foot facility are a reminder to students that they are not alone and represent the hope and opportunity that Boys Town offers its students. In addition to their symbolic meaning, the brick walls of the new Education Center offer superior insulation and soundproofing, which helps to create a calm and focused learning environment. The architect chose precast panels with thin brick due to time constraints, as it was taking the place of an older building that had to be torn down. Then the construction of the new building took its place. By using brick, the building was able to respect the iconic legacy of Boys Town. The new Education Center is a testament to Boys Town's commitment to providing its students with the best possible educational experience. The brick walls are a symbol of that commitment, and they will stand for decades to come, leaving behind a strong legacy of hope and strength.
